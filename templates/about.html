How Our Technology Works

Understanding the science behind DoseRight AI.

Warfarin is a highly effective anticoagulant, but its optimal dose varies dramatically between individuals. A dose that is therapeutic for one patient can be dangerous for another. This variability is largely influenced by a person's clinical factors (like age and weight) and their unique genetic makeup.

Our system uses a powerful machine learning model called a Random Forest Regressor. It has been trained on thousands of patient records to learn the complex, non-linear relationships between patient characteristics and the correct Warfarin dose. It operates in two modes: a Base Model for when only clinical data is available, and an Enhanced Model that incorporates genetic information from the CYP2C9 and VKORC1 genes for superior accuracy.

For a clinician to trust an AI's recommendation, they need to understand its reasoning. Our tool integrates a state-of-the-art technique called SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations). This transparency transforms our model from a "black box" into a trustworthy decision-support tool.
